Giving to those who can't give back.

Sarah

Published on January 30th, 2026

I think my caregiver for my disabled sister and I would both agree that we live incredibly blessed lives. We are both extremely grateful for the way we live and we strive to not leave anything in our lives unappreciated.


"God rewards the people who take care of the helpless. I think that's why we are so happy Tita. Because God blesses us for loving her." I texted her.


I truly believe God places people in our lives for a reason, and that taking care of someone else's needs is also a way of nurturing our own souls. Every act of kindness, patience, and love we give comes back to us in ways we can't always see.


It is a gift to love someone so graciously. I think of my sister and she is the pinnacle of what it means to love someone unconditionally. She is simply herself, and everyone around her is drawn to her without even saying a word. She has a sweet and innocent aura that can be felt by anyone.


Growing up alongside her has taught me true unconditional love, patience, and resilience. My service to her has taught me to see the world from a more joyful and compassionate lens, helping me recognize and appreciate great qualities in everyone. While she may not communicate with words, her loving actions and expressions have shown me a new depth of connection. And in every gesture and smile, she radiates a warmth that, no matter how hard I try, I can't possibly emulate as well as she does.


For her, I am endlessly grateful.
