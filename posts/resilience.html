Resilience is the backbone behind an effortlessly happy disposition.

Sarah

Published on March 9th, 2026

I was journaling the question what is one thing that makes you who you are last night. My first response was my natural optimism. If there is something in the world that I am the best at, it is finding the silver lining in any situation. I said that the cause for this was because I am naturally loving and grateful for everything in my life. My natural optimism has always felt like an inherited trait to me, and almost something whimsical and wishy-washy.


But as I kept journaling, I slowly uncovered that what the real foundation of who I was was my resilience. It was my strength to keep going in any situation. It was my radical acceptance of life’s challenges.


The word I repeated most in that journal entry? Trust.


I trust myself to pick myself up out of any situation and make myself happy. I trust myself to stay strong in any circumstance. I trust myself to be resilient. I trust myself to accept things the way they are instead of ruminating over what could’ve been.


My defining trait manifests as optimism and immense happiness on the outside, but it is resilience at its core. Resilience to me is the ability to adapt to life’s challenges well and quickly. Because I truly know myself to be a resilient human being at my core, I know I will handle anything that comes my way with grace. And when you accept that life will always happen to you no matter what, but you can control the way you respond — combined with the belief that everything happens for a reason — nothing can ever actually go wrong. Because I believe everything is for my highest good, and I will always respond in a way that reflects my highest self.


I wouldn’t know myself without my fortitude, courage, resilience, and strength. These traits are the essence of who I am.


What truly makes me who I am is my unwavering trust in my own resilience.
