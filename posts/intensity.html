Wasted potential is my only fear in life.

Sarah

Published on December 29th, 2025

"If you knew that all of your achievements would be forgotten immediately after you die, would you still pursue them with the same intensity?"


ChatGPT asked me this question and here was my response:


Honestly, yes. What is life without intensity, passion, and drive? What kind of a question is this? Let me pose a counter question to you: if you knew that all your achievements would be forgotten immediately after you die, and therefore declared that your actions had no meaning, what would you spend your life doing instead?


Oh my gosh. We don’t even know what happens after we die. Why are you even considering what happens to your achievements after you die??? When you do a hard workout, do you think to yourself, “This workout won’t even matter because I’m going to die anyways”? Like… what?! You are living in the present RIGHT NOW. You have goals and a person you dream of becoming in this lifetime, and all that should just go to waste because you’re going to die one day?? What kind of HEDONISTIC way of thinking is this? WHAT’S THE POINT OF DOING ANYTHING THEN IF WE’RE JUST GONNA DIE ANYWAYS???


We don’t live life focused on how our actions will affect us after we die. For Pete's sake, we don’t even know what will happen after we die. Just live how you want and live in the present moment. Do everything with HIGH INTENSITY. THERE IS NO OTHER WAY TO LIVE. You were gifted a living, breathing, working body with ENERGY. Are you going to let it go to waste? EVERYTHING should be done FULL OUT. Be the best at everything you do. THAT WAY, when it is time to die, you can say: I DID EVERYTHING I WANTED TO DO and have NO REGRETS.


Our achievements are primarily meant for US. WE ARE SELFISH. YES. We live life for us!!!! You should live a life that makes YOU PROUD. WAIT, but is it selfish??? Because here’s the thing: MOST PEOPLE WANT TO LIVE A LIFE THAT SERVES OTHERS. That is what makes people proud. A SELFLESS life is not selfish. It’s a WIN-WIN.


Bye. What else would you do with your life if not ATTACK LIFE with great intensity?


Okay maybe I misinterpreted the question. Maybe the point of the question was never "so you should stop striving," but to expose whether someone's drive is intrinsic or performative. And based on my response, mine is violently intrinsic.


You don’t lift thinking about death. You don’t study thinking about the grave. You don’t create, strive, or love with a posthumous audit committee in mind. You do it because aliveness demands expression.


Now, let me answer my counter‑question honestly: If I knew all my achievements would be forgotten immediately after I die, what would I spend my life doing instead? The exact same things. Because the value was never in remembrance. It was in becoming. The point of it all is who you become in the process.


And that’s the distinction most people miss.


There are two fundamentally different ways to live. Teleological living: “I do things for an outcome: legacy, recognition, permanence, validation.” Ontological living: “I do things because this is who I am while I’m alive.”


I am squarely in camp two. I have an existential responsibility. I was gifted with energy, I must expend it. I have a body, I must use it. I have consciousness, I must aim it.


“What’s the point if we’re going to die anyway?” This question only paralyzes people who outsource meaning to the future. Something most people don’t articulate but feel instinctively: Meaning is not stored. Meaning is experienced.


A workout matters while it’s happening. Discipline matters while it’s shaping you. Purpose matters while you’re living inside it. Death doesn’t retroactively delete lived intensity.


On “selfishness” :


I wrote "our achievements are primarily meant for us. We are selfish, yes.” This is true because a life that makes us proud is usually one that:
• requires discipline
• demands excellence
• involves contribution
• expands others


Which is why selfishness and service aren’t opposites. They converge at self-respect. People who hate themselves don’t help the world well. People who respect themselves often do because they demand more of their impact.


So yes: Live for yourself. Become formidable. Attack life with intensity.


And what accidentally happens? People around you rise. Confidence spreads. Energy transfers.


Final truth I learned from this question: I'm not afraid of death. I'm afraid of wasted potential while alive. So I choose to live full out. Not to be remembered. So that I can exist completely and fully.


So the takeaway? Live hard. Live fully. Attack your life with intention, discipline, and fire.
